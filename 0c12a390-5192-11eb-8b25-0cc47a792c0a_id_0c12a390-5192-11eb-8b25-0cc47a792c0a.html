The Importance of Personal Connection:
My erroneous vision of Phil Knight was that he was just an average businessman who
wanted to start up a business that produced fine running shoes. However, my assumption was
that Phil Knight’s career flowed smoothly like a river, that the result of it were treasures of
triumph and fame.
However, reading from many articles proved my hypothesis wrong. It turns out that Phil Knight’s
career started off lacking money and a clear direction of his future career. Having to face many struggles
such as losing money and not turning into great agreements with other companies, Knight’s will power
started to diminish and fade away. Fortunately, his persevering heart made him never fail and keep his
fighting spirit.
Moving onto Phil Knight’s early life, he ironically used to be a runner on the track team,
at the University of Oregon. Before becoming a prosperous man, Phil Knight had a great
connection to people who were involved in the running shoe industry. One day, Phil Knight
came into a realization that his coach of the track team, Bill Bowerman “tinkered” with athletes’
running shoes. During his 24 years as coach at the University of Oregon, Bowerman used to train 31
Olympic athletes and had such a big passion for running. Both having the same interest and goals, they
turned into an agreement and wanted to have a job in the part of the athletics department. Then, the two
teamed up and decided to create a company selling their idea of a “perfect” running shoe.
Given such an opportunity to have a big boost in his career, Phil Knight obtained the
mindset of a “shoe dog.” It all got him started when he saw the Tiger Sneakers (now known as
Asics) being produced in Japan. Fortunately, Phil Knight got a partnership deal with the Japanese
company and started selling the Tiger Shoes in the U.S. After selling $8,000 in 1964, they were on the
right track to success, earning a total of 1 million worth of Tigers. But after a while, Phil Knight had to
change the company’s foundation because there were struggles in the exclusivity agreement. He then
changed the company’s name to Nike after the goddess Nike. This goddess was known as “one of the
most courageous and chivalrous warriors at the dawn of civilization.” Her sport-like persona caught
Knight and Bowerman’s attention, therefore they named their company after her. Soon after the name,
Caroline Davidson created the famous “swoosh” as an emblem for Nike and was given $35 for it. After
releasing the business to the public, the sales rose dramatically from $10 to $270. This became the start of
an enormous company that changed the lives of many athletes all over the world. Today, the company
controls a shocking 62% of the U.S brand share. This means that Nike, which leads the industry with $28
billion in annual sales, could see the business boom.
The Takeaway Message:
Even though Knight himself is no longer the CEO of Nike and will step down as
chairman of the board of trustees this year, shareholders continue to be rewarded by the
incredibly well-performing company he started. Nike nearly doubled its net income in the past
five years, even 35 years after going public. While it’s always a concern when a founder leaves the
company he or she created, one last important takeaway from the memoir is that even from the beginning,
Knight was able to find incredibly talented people to operate the company with him. The past few years
have been no exception, so Nike should be just fine even as Knight moves on.
But as I show my perspective of Nike’s success, I’ve personally learned memorable
insights from Phil Knight. Finding your true passion and fulfilling it to do such a big thing just
like Phil Knight did is such a dream we should all conquer one day. Despite the obstacles and
hardships we face, if the word ‘terrible foe’ that is a voice in your head that tells you, “You
can’t”. To that, you should respond: “Just do it.”
